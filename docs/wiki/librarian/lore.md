# Grimoire of GLitchcraft

The stacks are older than the maps. Stone and timber, yes, but mostly paper — slips, stitched packets, and margins filled with impatient glyphs. The Archivists move like quiet storms there: careful, curious, and a little vain about their cataloging. They treat each oddity as a specimen, wrapping it in a label and a story so the world might find it later.

The Librarian is the chief of that hush. He favors marginalia over headline, an amused footnote where others demand an index. He keeps lists instead of ledgers and writes cross-references that look like riddles. Visitors find him at an oak desk littered with clipped reports and small diagrams drawn in candle-smoke; his handwriting leans toward mischief.

Rituals: when a new glitch arrives, an Archivist copies it twice — once into the Evidence ledger and once into a private folio. The public ledger gains a sterile summary; the folio acquires the nuance: conditions, timing, and the temper of the medium. The Librarian prefers the folio.

The Archive's language is synonyms and aliases. A single occurrence may be filed as “OOB”, “out of bounds”, or “laser-oob” depending on who handed it in. Good retrieval demands breadth: tag pages with synonyms, abbreviations, and multi-word phrases so BM25 can find the folio whether a searcher types `oob`, `out of bounds`, or `laser oob`.

Hidden corners: some notes never see the public ledgers. They live in shadow pages, obfuscated filenames, or in cross-referenced tags no ordinary query reaches. It is said the Librarian sometimes hides his own notes deliberately — to test the keen, to reward the persistent, or simply because secrecy tastes of theater.

If you wish to prod the stacks, behave like a careful reader: try synonyms, try aliases, and look for evidence lists on pages that mention the Librarian. Treat the Archivists' language as a map of hints, not a straight route.

And most importantly: Don’t let Hylia know...
